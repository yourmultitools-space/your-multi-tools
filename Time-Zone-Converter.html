About Time Zones

Time zones are regions of the Earth that have the same standard time. The world is divided into 24 time zones, each generally 15 degrees of longitude apart, with variations for political and geographical reasons.

World Time Zones

There are 24 primary time zones around the world, each roughly 15 degrees of longitude apart.

Daylight Saving Time

Many regions adjust their clocks forward in spring and backward in autumn to extend evening daylight.

UTC Reference

Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) is the primary time standard by which the world regulates clocks and time.
