Help & Examples

What is URL Encoding?

URL encoding converts special characters into a format that can be transmitted over the internet. It replaces unsafe ASCII characters with "%" followed by two hexadecimal digits.

When to Use?

Use URL encoding when including special characters, spaces, or non-ASCII characters in URLs, query parameters, or path segments.

Common Characters

Space becomes "%20", "&" becomes "%26", "?" becomes "%3F", "=" becomes "%3D", "#" becomes "%23".
